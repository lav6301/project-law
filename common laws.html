Common Laws

About Common Law

Common law, also known as case law, is a body of unwritten laws based on legal precedents established by the courts.
Common law draws from institutionalized opinions and interpretations from judicial authorities and public juries.
Common laws sometimes prove the inspiration for new legislation to be enacted.

Importance for Understanding:

It provides a flexible framework for addressing a wide range of legal situations.

Precedent and Stare decisis:

Common law relies heavily on precedent, which means decisions made in earlier cases serve as a guide for future cases.
The principle of "stare decisis" (to stand by things decided) ensures consistency and predictability in legal rulings.
