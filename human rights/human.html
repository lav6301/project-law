HUMAN RIGHTS LAW


Human rights law encompasses a set of legal principles and standards designed to protect and promote the inherent dignity and fundamental rights of every individual . In India, the protection of human rights is primarily governed by the Protection of Human Rights Act, 1993.

Protection of Human Rights Act, 1993

This act establishes the National Human Rights Commission (NHRC) at the national level and State Human Rights Commissions (SHRCs) at the state level. It outlines the functions and powers of these commissions, empowering them to inquire into human rights violations and recommend measures for their prevention.

Purpose and Objectives:

National Human Rights Commission (NHRC):

The Act establishes the NHRC to protect and promote human rights. Functions: Investigating complaints of human rights violations, conducting inquiries, and promoting human rights literacy.

State Human Rights Commissions (SHRCs):

Objective: Each state has an SHRC to address human rights issues at the state level. Functions: Inquiring into complaints, conducting research, and spreading human rights awareness.

Human Rights Courts:

Objective: These courts are set up to fast-track cases related to human rights violations. Functions: Ensuring speedy trials and effective justice in human rights cases

Human Rights Protection under the Act:

The Act defines human rights to include the right to life, liberty, equality, and dignity. It prohibits various forms of discrimination and ensures protection against torture and inhuman treatment.




HUMAN RIGHTS LAW & ITS PURPOSE & USES FOR COMMON PEOPLE

HUMAN RIGHTS LAW

Purpose of Human Rights Law:

Dignity and Equality:

The primary purpose is to safeguard the inherent dignity and equality of all individuals, irrespective of factors such as race, gender, religion, or socio-economic status.

Prevention of Abuse:

Human rights laws aim to prevent and redress abuses, atrocities, and injustices, promoting a society where individuals are protected from discrimination, torture, and other forms of mistreatment.

Justice and Accountability:

These laws establish mechanisms to hold perpetrators accountable for human rights violations, ensuring that justice is served and victims have avenues for seeking redressal.

Freedom and Civil Liberties:

Human rights laws guarantee fundamental freedoms and civil liberties, including the right to life, liberty, and security of person; freedom of expression; and the right to a fair trial. Establishes the National Commission for Protection of Child Rights (NCPCR). Aims to protect and promote the rights of children.

Uses for Common People:

Legal Recourse:

Human rights laws provide individuals with a legal framework to seek redressal when their rights are violated, offering avenues for complaint and investigation.

Advocacy and Awareness:

These laws empower individuals and organizations to advocate for human rights, raise awareness about violations, and work towards creating a culture of respect for human dignity.

Social Justice:

Human rights principles contribute to social justice by challenging discrimination and promoting inclusivity, fostering a society where everyone can live with dignity and equality.
