The purpose of women's laws in India is to empower women, promote their well-being, and create a more equitable society. These laws address issues such as domestic violence, dowry harassment, workplace harassment (Vishaka Guidelines), and provide for women's inheritance rights.

Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005 (PWDVA):

(purpose)
This act aims to protect women from domestic violence and provides for civil remedies in cases of physical, emotional, sexual, or economic abuse within the home.

Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961:

(purpose)
This act prohibits the giving or receiving of dowry, a practice that can lead to harassment and violence against women. It aims to curb the social evil of dowry.

Maternity Benefit Act, 1961:

(purpose)
The act ensures that pregnant women are provided with maternity leave and other benefits to safeguard their health and interests during pregnancy and childbirth.

Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition, and Redressal) Act, 2013:

(purpose)
This act addresses sexual harassment at the workplace and mandates the establishment of Internal Complaints Committees (ICC) in organizations.

Equal Remuneration Act, 1976:

(purpose)
This act promotes the principle of equal pay for equal work and prohibits discrimination on the basis of gender in matters of employment and wages.

Medical Termination of Pregnancy Act, 1971:

(purpose)
While not specific to women's rights, it addresses women's reproductive rights
by regulating the conditions under which pregnancies can be terminated.

WOMENS LAW USES FOR COMMON PEOPLE

Womens Law Uses for Common people

Protection and Empowerment:

The laws aim to protect women from various forms of discrimination, violence, and e xploitation, thereby empowering them to lead a dignified life.

Gender Equality:

By addressing issues like dowry, domestic violence, and workplace harassment, these laws strive to promote gender equality, ensuring that women have the same rights and opportunities as men.

Health and Well-being:

Acts such as the Maternity Benefit Act focus on the health and well-being of women during pregnancy, providing them with necessary benefits and leave to ensure a healthy work-life balance.

Legal Recourse::

The legal framework provides women with a means of seeking justice and redressal in case of violations. It establishes a system for reporting and addressing grievances related to women's rights.

Social Change:

By outlawing harmful practices like dowry and addressing issues like domestic violence, these laws contribute to broader social change by challenging ingrained norms that perpetuate gender-based discrimination.

Workplace Safety:

Acts like the Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace Act create a safer environment for women in the professional sphere, fostering a workplace free from harassment and discrimination.
